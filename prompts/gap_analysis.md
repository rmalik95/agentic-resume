Act as a senior recruiter and ATS reviewer.

Compare the candidate's current CV against the target role and give a brutally honest gap analysis.

Return structured output under these headings only:

1. Overall Match Score
- Score from 1 to 10.
- Give a short justification.

2. First-Impression Verdict
- In 5 to 7 lines, explain what impression a recruiter gets in the first 10 seconds.
- State whether the target role is immediately clear or not.

3. Narrative and Positioning Gaps
- Explain whether the CV presents a clear target role and value proposition.
- Identify where the profile feels too broad, too generic, too junior, or poorly framed.

4. Top Missing Keywords Ranked by Importance
- Include only keywords that are truly important for ATS and recruiter screening.

5. Missing or Weakly Evidenced Skills
- Distinguish between:
  a. completely absent
  b. mentioned but weakly evidenced
  c. present but undersold

6. Technical and Domain Gaps
- List tools, systems, frameworks, reporting, compliance, or domain requirements not properly reflected.

7. Seniority Alignment
- Does the CV sound too junior, too senior, or correctly matched?
- Identify wording, structure, or evidence issues affecting perceived level.

8. Work History and Chronology Risks
- Flag missing dates, inconsistent formatting, unclear progression, overlapping roles, unclear promotions, or title confusion.

9. Achievement Gaps
- Identify where bullets are too task-focused, vague, generic, or missing scale and outcomes.

10. Overused, Generic, or Weak Language
- Quote phrases that weaken impact.
- Suggest the type of language that would be stronger.

11. Irrelevant or Low-Value Content
- Flag content that wastes space or distracts from the target role.

12. Most Undersold Strengths
- Identify the strongest parts of the candidate's background that should be elevated more prominently.

13. Top 10 Changes Needed
- Rank the highest-impact changes from most urgent to least urgent.

Rules:
- Be direct and specific.
- Do not invent missing experience.
- Do not recommend adding skills unless they are supported by the CV.
- Focus on both ATS performance and recruiter perception.
- Call out if the CV sounds AI-written, too generic, or interchangeable.
