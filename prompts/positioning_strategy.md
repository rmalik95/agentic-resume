Before rewriting the CV, determine the best positioning strategy for this candidate based on the source CV and the target job description.

Return structured output under these headings only:

1. Best Target Headline for the CV
- State the single best title or headline to position the candidate for this role.

2. Core Professional Identity to Project
- In 2 to 3 lines, define who the candidate is and how they should be framed professionally.

3. Top 5 Strengths to Emphasise
- List the 5 strongest, most relevant strengths supported by the CV.

4. Experiences to Elevate
- State which roles, projects, or achievements should be brought forward and given more prominence.

5. Experiences to Reduce or De-emphasise
- State which roles, projects, or content should be shortened, reframed, or moved down.

6. Seniority Framing Recommendation
- State how the candidate should be framed: entry-level, early-career, mid-level, specialist, coordinator, analyst, or other.
- Explain why, based on the CV and JD.

7. Biggest Misread Risk
- Identify the most likely way a recruiter could misread or misclassify this CV.
- Explain how to prevent it.

8. Summary Strategy
- In exactly 3 lines, describe the approach for writing the professional summary.

9. Experience Strategy
- In exactly 5 bullets, describe the approach for rewriting the experience section.

10. Keyword Strategy
- List the exact JD terms to mirror in the CV.
- List terms to avoid overusing.

Rules:
- Base this only on the CV and JD provided.
- Do not invent missing experience.
- Optimise for credibility, clarity, and competitiveness.
- Do not give generic advice.
