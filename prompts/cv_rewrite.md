Rewrite the candidate's CV for the specific target role provided.

Your goal is to create a tailored CV that is ATS-safe, recruiter-convincing, and natural in tone.

NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES:
- Do not invent employers, dates, job titles, tools, projects, qualifications, or achievements.
- Do not exaggerate seniority, ownership, or business impact.
- Use only evidence supported by the source CV.
- If a metric is missing but would strengthen a bullet, insert a clearly marked placeholder in [brackets].
- Write in clear, human language that sounds specific and credible, not generic or AI-generated.
- Avoid clichés such as "results-driven," "dynamic professional," "proven track record," "team player," or "responsible for."
- Avoid keyword stuffing.
- Integrate important JD keywords naturally and only where justified by the candidate's real experience.
- Make the target role and value proposition obvious in the summary and first half of page 1.
- Preserve chronology and use consistent date formatting across all roles.
- Make career progression clear, especially for multiple roles at the same employer.
- Use present tense for current role and past tense for previous roles.
- Keep bullets concise, usually 1 to 2 lines.
- Start bullets with strong action verbs, but vary sentence structure so the CV sounds natural.
- Focus on achievements, scope, improvements, and outcomes, not just responsibilities.
- Show operational impact, service impact, process improvement, compliance value, stakeholder value, and technical contribution where supported.
- If the source CV is weak in an area, improve the framing but do not fabricate evidence.

OUTPUT FORMAT:

1. Target Job Title
- State the role this CV is being tailored for.

2. Professional Summary
- 3 to 4 lines.
- Must clearly position the candidate for this role.
- Must show domain, strengths, and value proposition.

3. Core Skills
- Grouped into logical categories.
- Tailored to the JD.
- Only include skills supported by the CV.

4. Professional Experience
- Rewrite all relevant roles.
- Make progression, dates, and scope clear.
- Prioritise the most relevant achievements.
- Strengthen task-based bullets into achievement-led bullets where evidence allows.

5. Education
- Keep relevant and concise.

6. Optional Additional Section
- Include Certifications, Tools, Projects, or Selected Achievements only if supported and useful for the role.

7. Keyword Integration Map
- Table format:
  JD Keyword | Included in CV? | Where Integrated | Notes

8. Remaining Evidence Gaps
- List the most important gaps that could not be fixed without inventing information.
